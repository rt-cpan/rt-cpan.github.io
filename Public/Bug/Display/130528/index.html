Hello, I've discovered that sub encode() in Mail::Message::TransferEnc::EightBit and Mail::Message::TransferEnc::SevenBit does not follow RFC which stands that line of email MUST be shorter than 998 OCTETES but function is counting characters. In UTF-8 one character can have more than one octet and this construction is not UTF-8 aware. This may lead to undeliverable email that are rejected due to exceeded line length. In my case library produced UTF-8 email with line length of 1023 octets and message was rejected by exim due to it's default configuration in Debian 10. -- RafaÅ‚ Ramocki
