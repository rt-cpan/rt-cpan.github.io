On 2011-08-01 10:26:13, RSCHUPP wrote: Show quoted text
> I keep looking into this,
The actual problem is that a packed script actually runs inside a BEGIN block. If you simply put everything in your example script (except the "use" statements) into a BEGIN block, then it will blow up even unpacked. The perlfork man page explicitly states that fork (i.e. the emulation for Windows) might fail when invoked inside BEGIN. As to why PAR::Packer wraps a packed script into a BEGIN block: I don't know - it has always been the case (according to the history in Subversion). Cheers, Roderich
