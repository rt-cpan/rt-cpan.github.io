text/plain 959b
If the service uses 'fork()' to start a child process (implemented as a thread on Win32), when the child process ends the service change state to SERVICE_STOP_PENDING and shutdown. The consequence is that we can not implement a cron-like service. I'm attaching a test case: - PerlService.inf is the script to install the service (right click, "Install" from the Windows Explorer) and uninstall (use the Control Panel to uninstall "Perl service demo"). - test-PCDW.pl: the daemon Here is the output: Fri Sep 25 17:05:55 2009 Begin Fri Sep 25 17:05:56 2009 2 Fri Sep 25 17:05:57 2009 4 Fri Sep 25 17:05:58 2009 4 Fri Sep 25 17:05:59 2009 4 Fri Sep 25 17:06:00 2009 4 Child start Fri Sep 25 17:06:01 2009 4 Fri Sep 25 17:06:02 2009 4 Fri Sep 25 17:06:03 2009 4 Fri Sep 25 17:06:04 2009 4 Fri Sep 25 17:06:05 2009 4 Child end Fri Sep 25 17:06:06 2009 3 Fri Sep 25 17:06:07 2009 1 Fri Sep 25 17:06:07 2009 End -- Olivier MenguÃ© - http://o.mengue.free.fr/
