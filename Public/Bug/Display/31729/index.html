I've extrapolated the Sizer code and the Tester code, and I think it is better that way. Tester was a clever idea, but I think it is safer to test the actual modules, not a sub-class; neither could I see the point of adding it to global install, since it is only used for testing. For this reason I have added WizTestSettings to the test library. This is basically just a subroutine that populates the Wizard with the standard pages. I've also taken out the plethora of print, warns, and so on, that were there for debugging, and added Log4perl instead, with stubs to cover the case of it not being uninstalled. Today I even tested without Log4perl, to avoid a repeat of the horrendous FAILs of yesterday (GMT). Thanks for your work.
