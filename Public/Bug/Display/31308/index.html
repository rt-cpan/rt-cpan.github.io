Subject: graph equivalence depends on serialization ordering.
Download (untitled) / with headers
text/plain 751b
The rdf_eq() function seems to depend on the ordering of statements in the serialized RDF given as arguments. Since the underlying RDF graph isn't changing when statements are re- ordered in the serialization, this is a significant bug. I suspect the problem is caused by Redland's serializer assigning bnodes in an ordering based on the serialization, and this is causing problems in your graph equivalence checking. I've attached two test files that compare the same two graphs. On my MacBook Pro with perl 5.8.8 and Test::RDF 0.0.3, the first (test_rdfeq_pass.pl) passes the test, while the second (test_rdfeq_fail.pl) fails the test. The only difference between the two files is a re-ordering of nodes in the turtle in the variable $second.
