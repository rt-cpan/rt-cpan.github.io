Because United States Citizens are the most generous group of people in the history or mankind. For some may believe that… …Saving the oceans improves another’s life …Contributing to arts and culture improves another’s life …Giving canned goods to the food bank improves another’s life …Ensuring families have access to counseling improves another’s life …Investing in breast cancer research improves another’s life Regardless of one’s political persuasion, politics, theology, or method…generosity celebrates the diverse and innumerable ways others help others. These thoughts, actions, and generosities are the cornerstone of a foundation upon which civil society has been built for 4,000 years. Why has December been chosen to be National Giving Month? • LAST YEAR AMERICANS GAVE $417.2 BILLION TO CHARITY • ONE THIRD (31%) OF ANNUAL GIVING OCCURS IN DECEMBER • 12% OF ALL GIVING HAPPENS IN THE LAST 3 DAYS OF THE YEAR • 28% OF NONPROFITS RAISE 50% OF THEIR FUNDING IN DECEMBER Furthermore, neither Congress nor the Executive Branch has officially dedicated any of our twelve months to acts of volunteerism or charitable giving? December provides us a very special opportunity to celebrate those individuals, groups and organizations who invest their lives in charity. Americans of all walks of life are volunteering their time and giving of their resources to improve lives and the quality of living of others. Please take a few moments to add your endorsement so that December officially becomes National Giving Month. Sign today and make the world a better place. Our generosity demonstrates that even in divisive times our commitment to charity is secure. Charitable giving increases each year, signaling that our friends give according to their beliefs and interests, which are both diverse and wide-ranging.
