The Confusing World of DietsÂ© Part Two In Part 1 we talked about diets for weight control and some of the problems they have. Let's look at the other type of diets. These are the diets for health. Examples include diets for diabetics, for heart health, gluten free diets, allergy free diets and so on. While some of these are good diets for their purpose, they don't have the marketing hype behind them. So they don't get publicized as much. The only people interested in these special health diets are the people that have heart problems, insulin problems, allergic problems or digestive problems. One of the confusing issues that come up is the fact that eating a diet for health has nothing to do with weight control. You might think that eating a "healthy diet" would automatically help you lose weight. Nothing could be further from the truth. You can get fatter than a bear just before hibernation on a healthy diet. And you can get skinny as a rail on a diet full of refined carbohydrates. The simple fact is that you can get as fat as you want or as skinny as you want on any kind of diet. People have lost weight on 70% fat diets. People have gained weight on 70% protein diets. Why is this you might wonder? Because everyone seems to forget human physiology and science. Here is the number one thing you need to know about how to gain weight. You already know it, so this is only a reminder. If you eat more calories than you burn, you will gain weight. It doesn't matter if all you eat are celery and carrot sticks. If you eat more of them in calories than you burn each day you will gain weight. Period, end of story. So, if you do not want to gain weight, then you need to maintain a balance between calories coming in and calories being burned. It is as simple as that and it will never get more complicated than that. Okay, so that is how you keep from gaining weight. What if you are overweight? What do you do if you want to lose weight? Losing weight is a bit more complicated. The best solution is to not gain excess weight in the first place. However, as over 67% of the American population is overweight or obese already, it is necessary to know how to lose weight. Up to a point any type of weight loss diet you go on works. This is another key point that the diet and weight loss industry knows, but does not tell you. Every diet works for awhile. So you go on a diet, you lose a little weight and are feeling pretty good. Then the weight loss slows down drastically or stops altogether. This can be a few weeks to a few months depending on your own physiology and lifestyle. But, the weight loss stops. So you attempt to eat less and less. That is futile. At this point in time you will not lose more weight by eating less and less. The physiology behind this is a little complicated, however it all boils down to your body attempting to preserve itself. In other words you body wants to maintain the status quo. So if you are 20 to 50 pounds overweight and have been there for awhile your body wants to stay there. It does not want to lose weight because that is change. Even if it is healthier for you to lose those 20 or more pounds your body will resist. In part 3 of this series, you will learn what the diet i ndustry does to sucker you into buying another diet.
