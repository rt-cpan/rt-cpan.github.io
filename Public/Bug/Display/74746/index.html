But, really, there's no *requirement* for the initial individual(s) to do the entire hit. Right now, someone with just z/OS domain knowledge of "how to build things", how to navigate around the file system, what the local equivalent of "cd", "less" and "diff" are would get a long way. Just being able to report back with build failure output would let everyone else solve the initial problems, and get things to a much better state than they are now. This would be a heck of a lot better than what we have now, which is a lot of effectively dead code, some of which is actively getting in the way of progress. And there's an easy option on that, which no-one on the perl-mvs list would like, but if nothing else changes, it's going to be the only solution left on the table.
