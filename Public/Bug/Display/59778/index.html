Effective Power Risk Management
September 15-16, 2010 - Intercontinental Exchange/ICE, New York, NY
Presented by ICE Education & Energy Management Institute


Learn cutting-edge purchasing, risk management & hedging strategies
needed to succeed in the power markets.

 Get course details    Register

You're invited to join EMI for this comprehensive Power Risk Management training. You'll gain comprehensive exposure to risk management tools and strategies specific to the electricity markets. We will consider market fundamentals, key price and volatility drivers and cutting-edge trading techniques that preserve and enhance margins in the power markets. We will further consider credit and operational risk mitigation.

Within the context of a hypothetical utility, the course will thoroughly examine risk metrics, purchasing and risk management objectives, expert hedging strategies and policies. This will allow you to develop specific risk management strategies best suited to meet the objectives of your company as well as understand those of your competitors and customers. We will further discuss corporate policies, procedures, board resolutions, FASB 133 and 157 requirements, and checks and balances required that would meet the most diligent auditor’s requirements.

Topics covered during this course include:

  • What factors drive value, volatility and risk in the power markets?
  • What hedging tools are available to mitigate market risk?
  • How are generators best understood as Real Options?
  • How can load be understood as a Real Option?
  • How can we integrate generators, load and derivatives into an integrated power portfolio?
  • What are the various risk metrics and when should they be used?
  • How does FAS 133 and 157 influence policies and procedures?
  • What is the ISDA Master Agreement and how is it used?
  • How do we measure and manage credit risk?
  • How can risk management ‘best practices’ be applied?
  • What are the infrastructure requirements?
  • How do we put all of this together into a prudent risk management plan?
