Subject: Table parsing does not comply to S26
Damian, The table parsing in 0.000011 does not comply to S26. For instance, H1 | H2 ============ row1 | a row2 | b-c+d gets parsed as a three-columns table although S26 specifies that "Columns are separated by two or more consecutive whitespace characters, or by a vertical line (|) or a border intersection (+), either of which must be separated from any content by at least one whitespace character.". I have looked at the code and the problem is in _column_template() that does not require whitespace characters surrounding the column separator. I have rewritten this routine to comply to S26 and the table above is now parsed correctly. However, this breaks one test (table_header.t). I have looked at the test and I have the feeling that the test is incorrect: why should the cell be ' Superpower' and not 'Superpower'? So I have changed the test too... The result is the attached patch that should improve S26 compliance. Cheers, Lionel
